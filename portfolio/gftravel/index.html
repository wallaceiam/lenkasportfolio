GF Travel

Native Mobile App helping people with gluten alergy to fully experience international cuisine on their travels with no need to speak a local language

Project Overview

.

The Brief

  • Create a new native mobile app
  • Use full UX design process to define and design core functions and overall product architecture
  • Ensure travellers with gluten alergy are able to experience international cousine safely
  • Able to explain the food requirement and alergy in different languages
  • Educate individuals about gluten alergy
  • Very easy and simple to use in a few seconds
  • Focus on navigation, usability, and product value

The Problem

  • Promote awarness of gluten alergy and its consequences to any human being suffering from this condition
  • Ability to explain this alergy in any language without the need of speaking local language
  • Create a bridge of understanding between the travellers and local people while ordering food
  • Reduce stress and fear that is stopping an individual from being able to experience an international cousine on the travels (restaurants, pubs, markets, food stalls, etc.)
  • Being able to explain what an individual can and cannot eat
  • Create a travel companion that is available in offline mode.
  • Must be easy and quick to use on the go
  • Incorporate visuals
  • Promote freedom to experiment and experience travels without worries about food and limitations.

The Solution

GF Travel native mobile app acts as a travel companion for individuals who suffer from gluten alergy and are under pressure and fear to try local cousine and fully experience their travels. With an incorporation of full UX design process, this app has a quick and easy feature that illustrates what gluten alergy and its consequences are in many international languages. It also has a visual feature of what an individual can or cannot eat to make it easier while ordering food. GF Travel creates an effortless channel between travellers with gluten alergy and local people on the go and in offline mode.

Due to confidentiality, case study and prototype are not available for the public.
